## Overview

A machine-learning approach to **riverbank erosion detection and prediction**, built as part
of my research internship on the CAP-RES project at the International Centre for Climate
Change and Development (ICCCAD). Riverbank erosion is one of the most damaging slow-onset
climate hazards in Bangladesh, displacing communities and reshaping floodplains; data-driven
prediction helps target resilience and adaptation efforts.

## Key Features

- **Erosion detection:** ML methodology to identify erosion-prone riverbank segments.
- **Predictive modelling:** Models trained to anticipate erosion patterns to inform planning.
- **Climate-resilience focus:** Built to feed directly into adaptation and capacity-building
  work, bridging computational methods with field realities.

## Technologies Used

- **Python**, machine-learning and data-analysis libraries
- Geospatial / remote-sensing inputs for erosion mapping
