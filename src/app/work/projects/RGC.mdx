## Overview

RGC (Radio Galaxy Classifier) is a deep-learning pipeline for automatically classifying the
morphology of radio-loud active galactic nuclei (AGNs) from large-scale survey imagery. The
work focuses on **bent-tail radio galaxies** — specifically **Wide-Angle Tail (WAT)** and
**Narrow-Angle Tail (NAT)** sources — whose distorted morphologies trace their interaction
with the surrounding intracluster medium.

This project forms the basis of my undergraduate thesis, *"Machine Learning Approaches for
Radio Galaxy Morphology Classification,"* at the Center for Astronomy, Space Science and
Astrophysics (CASSA), Independent University, Bangladesh.

## Key Features

- **Equivariant CNNs (e2cnn):** Uses E(2)-equivariant steerable convolutions so the model
  is robust to the arbitrary rotations radio sources appear in — a natural fit for the
  rotational symmetry of astronomical images.
- **Semi-supervised learning:** Leverages a large pool of unlabelled survey cutouts
  alongside a small labelled set, addressing the chronic scarcity of expert-labelled
  radio-galaxy data.
- **VLA / LoTSS imagery:** Trained and evaluated on cutouts from large radio surveys to
  classify bent radio AGNs at scale.
- **Reproducible pipeline:** End-to-end data preparation, model training, evaluation, and
  inference designed for re-use on new survey releases.

## Technologies Used

- **Python**, **PyTorch**, and **e2cnn** for equivariant deep learning
- **Astropy** and **FITS** handling for astronomical image data
- **NumPy / Matplotlib** for analysis and visualization

## Research Output

This work is being prepared as a publication — *RGC: a radio AGN classifier based on deep
learning. I. A semi-supervised model for the VLA images of bent radio AGNs* (under review).
Ongoing work extends the same model to additional morphological classes (sFRI and sFRII).
