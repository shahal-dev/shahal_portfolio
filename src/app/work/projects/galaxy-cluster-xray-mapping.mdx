## Overview

A pipeline for **spatially-resolved spectroscopy** of galaxy clusters using Chandra X-ray
Observatory ACIS data. Galaxy clusters are the largest gravitationally bound structures in
the universe; their intracluster medium (ICM) — hot gas at 10⁷–10⁸ K — emits X-rays through
thermal bremsstrahlung. Mapping the temperature, pressure, and density of this gas reveals
cluster dynamics, merger shocks, and diffuse radio sources.

This work supports my research at CASSA on **detecting radio mini-haloes** in X-ray galaxy
cluster images and writing a reproducible pipeline to create temperature maps from Chandra
data. It builds on and extends the ClusterPyXT toolchain with a streamlined, GUI-free CLI
workflow.

## What It Does

- **Data reprocessing:** Reprocesses raw Chandra ACIS observations with CIAO/CALDB.
- **Deflaring & point-source removal:** Cleans flares and excludes contaminating point sources.
- **Observation merging:** Reprojects and merges multiple observations into a single
  exposure-corrected surface-brightness map.
- **Contour binning:** Adaptively bins regions to a target signal-to-noise for spectral fits.
- **Thermodynamic maps:** Produces temperature, pressure, and density maps of the ICM via
  spectral fitting across the cluster.

## Technologies Used

- **CIAO 4.14 / CALDB** — Chandra X-ray data reduction
- **Python 3.8+**, **Astropy**, **NumPy**, **Matplotlib**
- Contour-binning spectral analysis for thermodynamic mapping
